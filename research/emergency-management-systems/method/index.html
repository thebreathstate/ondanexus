Stage-Based Systems Performance Lens (GSPS)

A consistent stage model used to compare execution behavior across storms without changing the lens.

By Bob Pudlock, Onda Nexus • Published: 2026-02-08

Comparability Execution visibility Post-award focus

GSPS is used here as a practical structure for organizing observation and enabling cross-storm comparison. It is not a policy framework.

Why a stage lens

Large-scale disaster recovery involves many actors, timelines, and overlapping programs. When delivery slows or degrades, performance narratives often collapse multiple phases into one. A stage-based lens separates these phases so execution constraints are not misattributed.

How GSPS is used in this project

  • Each storm is analyzed with the same stages and the same questions.
  • Observation is separated from interpretation.
  • Special attention is placed on post-award execution: mobilization, throughput, quality, cycle time.
  • Where possible, oversight signals are summarized from public reporting and audits.

GSPS stages (v1)

Stage What it covers Boundary signal
1) Event & Immediate Response Impact, life safety operations, emergency sheltering, initial FEMA response Life safety stabilizes
2) Stabilization & Interim Housing Temporary housing, basic repairs, FEMA IA/PA overlap, debris clearance, insurance progression Long-term recovery formally initiates
3) Program Design & Capitalization Needs assessments, supplemental appropriations, CDBG-DR allocation, Action Plan creation/approval Funds become available to obligate
4) Vendor Mobilization & Intake Procurement, onboarding, intake pipelines, DOB checks, environmental review, permitting initiation Execution becomes practically available
5) Production & Delivery Construction/repair, inspections, payments/draws, QA/QC, completion Stable throughput and quality
6) Closeout, Oversight & Learning Closeout milestones, audits/reviews, corrective actions, lessons learned Institutional learning codified

What the lens makes visible

  • Execution constraints in Stage 5 often originate in Stage 3–4 readiness conditions.
  • Vendor performance behaves like a downstream signal of intake discipline, inspection cadence, permitting flow, and payment mechanics.
  • Cross-storm comparison helps distinguish storm-specific issues from recurring system behavior.

Attribution

This work relies on publicly available materials such as program updates, official reporting, and publicly released oversight findings. As the library expands, individual pages may include direct links to source documents where they materially inform a specific observation.
